1

Configure firmware

Pick a version + your board, set the region and (optionally) a bind phrase. It downloads the matching pre-built firmware and personalises it in your browser (the bind phrase never leaves this page). No GitHub account or token needed.

Two slots, kept side by side. Staging v3 into app0 and v4 into app1 does not erase the other — both firmwares live on the board and you choose which one boots (Step 3 → Active slot, or 3 quick power-cycles with the slot-switch bootloader). Stage one slot to keep the other untouched, or stage both to load v3 and v4 together.

2

Connect your board

Plug the board in over USB and connect. Hold BOOT while connecting if it won’t sync. Flashing tools unlock once connected.

3

Flash to the board

Flash the firmware you staged (from the cloud)

Use these after Step 1.
Provision both sets up a new/stock board — it writes the bootloader, partition table and both slots (needs both v3 and v4 staged), then boots app0. Update appN replaces just that one version, leaving the other slot, the bootloader and the active-slot choice untouched.

Tools

⇄

Slot switcher

Choose which firmware boots. Read active shows the current one.

⚙

Slot-switch bootloader

Installs a custom second-stage bootloader (to 0x1000 only; app slots untouched). Then 3 quick power-cycles (each off/on within ~2 s) flip the active slot — no computer needed. Normal boots wait ~2 s. 4 MB ESP32 / min_spiffs.
